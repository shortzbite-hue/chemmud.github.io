Combining them here triggers a simulated flash, shake, and bang as a safety warning, not an instruction. Never try these outside a professionally supervised demonstration.

About this bench: outcomes here are simplified, game-style simulations built for exploring general chemistry concepts (acids, bases, indicators, gases, precipitates, and the periodic table) — they are not lab-accurate procedures, and quantities/temperatures are illustrative, not real yields. A few real-world pairs (marked in red) get a safety note instead of an animation, because they're genuinely hazardous if actually combined and worth knowing about. Nothing on this page tells you how to make or scale up anything — for real demonstrations, watch the linked videos or ask a teacher to show you in person.
